Body Analysis 6/10

Framework Checks (SOP_Scripting.md — Parts 2, 3, 4, 6)

One Sale Rule: The video stays focused on one core problem (reacting to everything) and one promise (emotional bulletproofing), maintaining a clear arc without drifting (SOP_Scripting.md PART 4).
Body type: Hybrid (most resembles Type 2 - Instructing Bullet Points, but with some specific advice).
Story structure: None — a 'Contrarian Reframe' structure would fit best.
Transitions: The transitions are present but very minimal, often just a new statement without a clear bridge between ideas. For example, 'Here is the first shift you need to make today' is a transition, but the flow between 'data' and 'detaching from outcome' could be smoother (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).
Specificity: The advice is generally actionable ('Stop treating every setback like a personal attack and start treating it like data'), but it lacks specific examples or client stories to illustrate the points, making it less impactful (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).
Tone: The tone is human and direct, using short sentences and addressing the viewer directly with 'you.' There are no obvious AI-tone patterns like abstract nouns or corporate connectors (SOP_Scripting.md PART 3).
Awareness level: This video targets a problem-aware audience (Stage 2) who know they react too much and feel stuck, but don't know the solution (SOP_Scripting.md PART 4). This is correctly matched.
Persuasion mechanics: The body primarily pushes the 'EASY' emotion by framing setbacks as 'data' and suggesting a simple 'shift.' It also hints at 'SAFE' by implying a predictable path to better performance. It could benefit from emphasizing the 'BIG/FOMO' of staying stuck (SOP_Scripting.md PART 2).

Line-Level Improvements

“[01:10] Stop treating every setback like a personal attack and start treating it like data”
This is good advice, but it's a bit abstract. It needs a concrete example to help the viewer immediately grasp how to apply it (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).
Think about a time you got a 'no' from a client, or a project didn't go your way. Did it feel like a personal attack? Instead, what if you just saw it as data? Like, 'Okay, that approach didn't work. What did I learn?'
“[02:10] When you detach from the outcome you actually perform better”
This is a strong claim that needs proof or a mini-story to make it more believable and impactful. Without it, it feels like generic advice (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).
I've seen this with clients who obsess over every sale. The moment they stopped needing the outcome so badly, and just focused on serving, their numbers actually went up. It's counter-intuitive, but when you detach, you perform better because you're not playing scared.

Verdict

The body has solid core ideas but lacks the specific examples and storytelling that make content truly sticky and actionable. Adding concrete illustrations and structuring it with a clear narrative like a 'Contrarian Reframe' would make the powerful concepts land harder (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).

RECOMMENDED FIX — STORY STRUCTURE
The 'Contrarian Reframe' structure would serve this video best. It could start by challenging the common wisdom of 'trying harder' or 'being positive' and then introduce the new framework of 'data' and 'detachment' as a superior approach, with practical application and proof (SOP_Scripting.md PART 6).
